The UAE's increasing reliance on digital infrastructure, particularly in sectors like finance, energy, and transportation, makes it highly vulnerable to sophisticated cyberattacks. A localized version of Glasswing could be adapted to protect these critical systems. A solo developer could start by researching the specific vulnerabilities of UAE's infrastructure, focusing on common attack vectors and compliance requirements like the UAE Information Assurance Standards (IAS). They could then build a proof-of-concept tool that analyzes code for these vulnerabilities, integrating with existing security tools used by UAE government entities and private companies. While international cybersecurity firms operate in the UAE, a locally-focused solution with Arabic language support and understanding of regional threat landscapes would have a competitive edge.

The investment in ShengShu, an AI video generator, signals a strong market demand. This could be replicated in the UAE to cater to the growing need for localized content in Arabic and English. The UAE's vibrant marketing and advertising sector, coupled with its focus on education and e-learning, creates a strong market for AI-generated video content. A solo developer could begin by training an AI model on a dataset of UAE-specific content, including local dialects, cultural nuances, and popular landmarks. The model could then be used to generate short, engaging videos for marketing campaigns, educational tutorials, or social media content. Existing video production companies in the UAE could be considered competition, but an AI-powered solution offering faster turnaround times and lower production costs would be highly attractive.
